Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

This dataset collects information from 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment.

Data Dictionary

  • PatientId: Identification of a patient
  • AppointmentID: Identification of each appointment
  • Gender: Male or Female . Female is the greater proportion, woman takes way more care of they health in comparison to man.
  • ScheduledDay: The day of the actuall appointment, when they have to visit the doctor.
  • AppoinmentDay: The day someone called or registered the appointment, this is before appointment of course.
  • Age: How old is the patient.
  • Neighbourhood: Where the appointment takes place.
  • Scholarship: True of False . Observation, this is a broad topic, consider reading this article https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bolsa_Fam%C3%ADlia
  • Hipertension: True or False
  • Diabetes: True or False
  • Alcoholism: True or False
  • Handcap: True or False
  • SMS_received: 1 or more messages sent to the patient.
  • No-show: True or False.

Question(s) for Analysis¶

What factors are important for us to know in order to predict if a patient will show up for their scheduled appointment?

  • Is there a correlation between gender and the likelihood of a no-show in health appointments?
  • Which age group does not show up for their appointments most?
  • How is the gap between scheduled and appoinment day impact on no show rate?
  • Are the patients with hipertension/scholarship/alcoholism/diabets less proportion to don't show up for their appointments than the others?

def plot_stack(factor, ax=None):
    '''
    docstring: Create a stacked chart to show statistic of given factor and show/no-show rate
    inputs: factor
    output:...
    '''
    group_df = df.groupby(factor)['no_show'].value_counts(normalize=True).mul(100).unstack()
    
    if not ax:
        fig, ax = plt.subplots(nrows=1,ncols=1, figsize=[14,5]);
    
    plot = group_df.plot(kind='bar', ax=ax, stacked=True, rot=0)
    
    for col in group_df:
        for i, value in enumerate(group_df[col]):
            plot.text(i, 100 - value, f'{value:.2f}%', ha='center', va='bottom', color='white', weight='bold')

    plot.set_title(f'Percentage show/no-show: {factor}'.title(), fontsize=12, weight='bold')
    plot.set_ylabel('Percentage')
    plot.set_xlabel(factor.title())
    plot.legend(labels=['Show Up', 'No Show'])
    
def plot_line(factor):
    '''
    docstring: Create a line chart to distribution of factor
    inputs: factor
    output:...
    '''
    grouped_df = df.groupby([factor, 'no_show']).size().unstack()

    absence_rate_percentage = (grouped_df['Yes'] / grouped_df.sum(axis=1)) * 100

    # Plotting the bar chart with percentage labels
    fig, ax = plt.subplots(figsize=(20, 6))
    absence_rate_percentage.plot(kind='line', ax=ax)

    # Customizing the plot if needed
    plt.title('Percentage of No Show Rate by {}'.format(factor), fontsize=16, weight='bold')
    plt.xlabel(factor.capitalize())
    plt.ylabel('No Show Percentage')
    plt.xticks(range(df[factor].min(), df[factor].max(), 5))
    plt.axhline(y=absence_rate_percentage.mean(), color="Green", linestyle='--', label='Average No Show Percentage')

    # Display the plot
    plt.show()
    
def plot_pie(df, factor, palette=None, explode=[0,0.1], ax=None, labels=None):
    '''
    docstring: Create a pie chart to statistic of given factor
    inputs:...
    output:...
    '''
    if not labels:
        labels=[f'Without {factor}', f'With {factor}']
    if not ax:
        fig, ax = plt.subplots(nrows=1,ncols=1, figsize=[14,5]);
    plot = df[factor].value_counts().plot(kind='pie', ax=ax, explode=explode, autopct='%1.2f%%',
                                         labels=labels,
                                         startangle=90, counterclock=False)
    plot.set_title(f'Distribution of {factor}'.title(), fontsize=12, weight='bold')
    plot.set_ylabel('')
    plot.axis('equal')
    plot.legend(title=factor.title());

Conclusions¶

Based on my exploratory data analysis (EDA) of the dataset, I have identified several variables that exhibit associations, to some extent, with the likelihood of patients showing up for their appointments:

  • There is no significant distinction between male and female patients in terms of not showing up for their appointments. The proportions of non-attendance are similar for both genders (20.31% and 19.98%), indicating that gender alone does not play a differentiating role in predicting no-shows.

  • Both toddlers (0-5 years old) and seniors (over 45 years old) exhibit a higher likelihood of patients showing up for their appointments with the absence rate below medium (20%) compared to other age groups. This can be attributed to the fact that people tend to prioritize their health conditions more during these particular stages of life.

  • It is important to minimize the waiting time for appointments. Prolonged waiting periods may result in patients either recovering or forgetting about their scheduled appointments.

  • Furthermore, there are some noteworthy findings that require further attention:

    • Surprisingly, patients who received SMS reminders for their appointments had a higher likelihood of missing their appointments. Thus, allocating resources and time towards sending SMS notifications may not have a positive effect, but instead have a negative impact. Budget and resources can be saved by reconsidering the use of SMS reminders.

    • Individuals enrolled in the special Bolsa Familia program or those with the Scholarship variable marked as TRUE showed a higher (4%) tendency to not show up for their appointments.

One limitation of this analysis is the unequal distribution between the female and male populations, as well as potentially other factors.

To address this issue and mitigate its impact, it would be beneficial to obtain a larger sample size to achieve a more balanced representation across the various factors under consideration. By obtaining more data, we can aim to fill the gaps in the distribution and improve the overall validity and reliability of the analysis.
